## 2. Professional summary (150 words)

Backend engineer focused on **transactional systems**, **payment integrity**, and **identity and access** in production environments. Experience designing and operating booking and reservation platforms with strict payment flows: multiple gateways (Stripe, Mercado Pago, Pix), webhook handling with HMAC validation and idempotency, and promotion engines with transactional claims and concurrency-safe limits. Built **government SSO** for a municipality: unified citizen login across services, identity validation against national registries (Mi Argentina, RENAPER, AFIP), and role-based access control. Comfortable with **modular monolith** architecture, external system synchronization (catalog and reservations), and clear domain boundaries. Stack: PostgreSQL, Django REST Framework, Docker, CI/CD. Interested in fintech, govtech, and SaaS backends where correctness, auditability, and security are non-negotiable.

## 3. Six capability pillars

| Pillar | What it signals |
|--------|------------------|
| **Transactional systems** | Booking and reservation platforms; state machines; idempotency keys; consistency between reservations, payments, and inventory. |
| **Payments & gateways** | Stripe, Mercado Pago, Pix; webhooks as source of truth; HMAC validation; idempotency by event_id; atomic updates (payment + booking state). |
| **Identity & access** | Government SSO; unified login across services; validation against Mi Argentina, RENAPER, AFIP; RBAC; multi-tenant and role-scoped authorization. |
| **Architecture** | Modular monolith; bounded contexts (reservations, payments, promotions, catalog); interfaces between domains; evolution without distributed operational overhead. |
| **Integrations & sync** | External catalog and reservation sync; normalized models and mapping tables; eventual consistency; retry and backoff; chunked jobs. |
| **Delivery & ops** | PostgreSQL, Django REST Framework; Docker; CI/CD; production concerns: concurrency, observability, safe deployments. |
